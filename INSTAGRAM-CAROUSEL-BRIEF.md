## 2. NON-NEGOTIABLE COMPLIANCE RULES (Florida notary law)

These are hard rules. Apply them to every slide, caption, and hashtag.

1. **Not an attorney — never give or imply legal advice.** Pete Quintero is a Florida
   notary public, **not** an attorney. Never say or imply the business can give legal
   advice, choose which document or notarial certificate a client needs, draft legal
   documents, or interpret documents. Avoid words like "legal advice," "we'll tell you
   which form," "legally binding because we said so."

2. **Never use "notario" or "notario público," and never literally translate
   "Notary Public" into Spanish.** Florida law (s. 117.05(11)) prohibits a non-attorney
   notary from using these terms or a literal translation in advertising, because in many
   countries a *notario* is an attorney. **In Spanish content, keep the term
   "Notary Public" in English** (e.g. "Notary Public móvil en Miami"). Do **not** use
   hashtags like #notario or #notariopublico.

3. **Any non-English ad must carry the statutory disclaimer**, in English *and* in the
   ad's language, in a conspicuous size:
   > "I AM NOT AN ATTORNEY LICENSED TO PRACTICE LAW IN THE STATE OF FLORIDA, AND I MAY NOT
   > GIVE LEGAL ADVICE OR ACCEPT FEES FOR LEGAL ADVICE."

   Spanish version:
   > "NO SOY UN ABOGADO LICENCIADO PARA EJERCER LA ABOGACÍA EN EL ESTADO DE LA FLORIDA, Y NO
   > PUEDO DAR ASESORÍA LEGAL NI ACEPTAR HONORARIOS POR ASESORÍA LEGAL."

   Put it on the final slide (and/or in the caption) of any Spanish-language carousel, and
   include the notary fees that may be charged (see Section 5).

4. **Tell the truth — only real credentials.** Use only the facts in Section 5. No
   exaggeration, no fake reviews, no "#1" or "best in Miami" claims, no guaranteeing legal
   outcomes or that a document will be accepted by any third party.

5. **Fees must be accurate.** Florida caps notarial acts at **$10 per act** and **$30 to
   solemnize a marriage** (Ch. 117). Travel/after-hours fees are **separate, agreed in
   advance, and not set by the state** — always frame them that way. Never imply the $10
   includes travel.

6. **No immigration or legal-service claims.** Do not advertise immigration help, form
   selection, or anything beyond notarization and the listed services.

7. **Protect privacy — never depict real documents or IDs.** No real names, signatures,
   account numbers, SSNs, ID cards, or client documents in graphics. Use generic mockups
   or abstract icons only.

8. **Always brand the post.** Include the handle/logo so credit survives reposts, and keep
   any stated service area accurate (Miami-Dade & Broward).

When in doubt, choose the more conservative, clearly-truthful option.

## 4. Instagram carousel best practices

**Canvas & specs**
- Use **4:5 portrait, 1080 × 1350 px** (takes the most feed space). 1:1 (1080×1080) is the
  fallback. Pick one ratio per carousel and keep it consistent.
- Up to 20 slides are allowed; **the sweet spot is 5–8.** Every slide must earn its place.
- Export high-res PNG or JPG, RGB.
- **Safe zone:** keep all critical text/logos ~120–150 px inside every edge. Keep the
  bottom ~250 px lighter on key content (handle, captions, and the dot indicator overlap
  there).

**Structure**
- **Slide 1 = the hook.** It's a thumbnail and a scroll-stopper. One bold promise or
  curiosity gap in big Fraunces type + a "Swipe →" cue. Must read at a glance.
- **Middle slides = one idea each.** ≤ ~15–20 words per slide. Headline + one short
  supporting line. Use numbers/steps ("1 of 4") for momentum.
- **Final slide = the CTA.** Tell them exactly what to do: *Book a signing*, *Call/Text
  (786) 634-0070*, *Save this*, *Share with someone closing soon*, *Link in bio.* Include
  logo/handle and (for Spanish posts) the disclaimer.

**Design consistency**
- Same background, grid, margins, and type styles on every slide — only the content
  changes. Number the slides.
- Strong contrast: off-white or light-gold text on charcoal. Never gold text on gold.
- Add gentle continuity cues (an arrow, a gold rule line, or an element bleeding to the
  next slide) so people keep swiping.
- One accent idea per slide; don't crowd. White space reads as "premium."
- Put a small handle/logo watermark in a bottom corner of each slide.

**Accessibility**
- Large, legible type (headline ≥ ~60px on a 1080-wide canvas; body ≥ ~34px).
- Provide **alt text** for each slide (describe the text + key visual).
- Don't rely on color alone to convey meaning.
