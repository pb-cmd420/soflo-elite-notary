## 2. NON-NEGOTIABLE COMPLIANCE RULES (Florida notary law)

Hard rules. Apply to every slide, caption, and hashtag.

1. **Not an attorney — never give or imply legal advice.** Pete Quintero is a Florida
   notary public, **not** an attorney. Never imply the business gives legal advice, chooses
   which document or certificate a client needs, drafts legal documents, or interprets
   documents.

2. **Never use "notario" or "notario público," and never literally translate
   "Notary Public" into Spanish** (Fla. Stat. 117.05(11)). In Spanish content, keep the term
   **"Notary Public" in English** (e.g. "Notary Public móvil en Miami"). Do **not** use
   #notario or #notariopublico.

3. **Any non-English ad must carry the statutory disclaimer**, in English *and* in the ad's
   language, conspicuously, plus the notary fees that may be charged:
   > EN: "I AM NOT AN ATTORNEY LICENSED TO PRACTICE LAW IN THE STATE OF FLORIDA, AND I MAY
   > NOT GIVE LEGAL ADVICE OR ACCEPT FEES FOR LEGAL ADVICE."
   > ES: "NO SOY UN ABOGADO LICENCIADO PARA EJERCER LA ABOGACÍA EN EL ESTADO DE LA FLORIDA,
   > Y NO PUEDO DAR ASESORÍA LEGAL NI ACEPTAR HONORARIOS POR ASESORÍA LEGAL."

4. **Tell the truth — only real credentials.** Use only Section 5 facts. No exaggeration,
   no fake reviews, no "best/#1," no guaranteeing legal outcomes or third-party acceptance.

5. **Fees must be accurate.** Florida caps notarial acts at **$10/act** and **$30 to
   solemnize a marriage**. Travel/after-hours fees are **separate, agreed in advance, not set
   by the state** — always frame them that way. Never imply $10 includes travel.

6. **No immigration or legal-service claims.** Nothing beyond notarization and the listed
   services.

7. **Protect privacy — never depict real documents or IDs.** No real names, signatures,
   account numbers, SSNs, ID cards, or client documents. Generic mockups/icons only.

8. **Always brand the post** (handle/logo) and keep the service area accurate (Miami-Dade &
   Broward).

When in doubt, choose the more conservative, clearly-truthful option.

## 4. Instagram carousel best practices

**Canvas & specs**
- **4:5 portrait, 1080 × 1350 px** (max feed space). Use this ratio for every slide.
- **Every carousel is EXACTLY 6 slides — 6 images.** No more, no fewer.
- Export each slide as a high-res **PNG, RGB, 1080×1350**.
- **Safe zone:** keep critical text/logos ~120–150 px inside every edge; keep the bottom
  ~250 px lighter (handle, caption, and dot indicator overlap there).

**Fixed 6-slide structure** (always):
- **Slide 1 = the hook** (thumbnail + scroll-stopper): one bold promise or curiosity gap in
  big Fraunces type + a "Swipe →" cue. Must read at a glance.
- **Slides 2–5 = four content slides,** one idea each, ≤ ~15–20 words. Eyebrow label +
  headline + one short supporting line. If a topic has fewer than four points, split or add
  a supporting slide so there are always exactly four. If it has more, keep the strongest
  four.
- **Slide 6 = the CTA:** *Book a signing · Call/Text (786) 634-0070 · Save this · Share ·
  Link in bio.* Include logo/handle. (Spanish posts: add the Section-2 disclaimer + fees.)

**Design consistency**
- Same background, grid, margins, and type styles on every slide — only content changes.
- High contrast: off-white or light-gold on charcoal; never gold-on-gold.
- Continuity cues (arrow, gold rule, element bleeding to next slide) to keep them swiping.
- One accent idea per slide; white space = "premium." Handle/logo watermark in a bottom
  corner.

**Accessibility**
- Large legible type (headline ≥ ~60px, body ≥ ~34px on a 1080-wide canvas).
- Provide **alt text** per slide. Don't rely on color alone.
